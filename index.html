Proof of Fairness Engine

왜 블록 변조가 어렵고, 왜 동일 결과가 재현되는지 설명합니다
PUBLIC • DETERMINISTIC • REPLAYABLE

같은 블록 데이터와 같은 공식이면, 누구나 같은 결과를 다시 얻습니다.

Block Verify는 서버 내부 난수가 아니라 공개 블록 데이터를 입력값으로 사용합니다. 운영자가 값을 임의로 바꾸지 않는 한, 같은 블록 번호·Tx 수·Output 값·시간 정보는 항상 같은 계산 결과만 만듭니다.

Immutable Block Source No Hidden RNG Replay Same Result
01 · 왜 변조가 어려운가
확정 블록은 사후 조작이 매우 어렵습니다

블록 데이터는 네트워크에 공개 기록되며, 이미 확정된 블록을 특정 결과에 맞게 단독 수정하기 어렵습니다. 즉, 결과 재료 자체가 운영 서버 내부 값보다 훨씬 강한 공개 검증 기반을 가집니다.

02 · 핵심 의미
같은 입력값 = 같은 결과

Block Number, Tx Count, Output Value, Block Time을 같은 공식에 대입하면 항상 동일한 결과가 도출됩니다. 숨겨진 랜덤 시드나 관리자 전용 보정값이 끼어들지 않는다는 점이 핵심입니다.

03 · 이 기능의 의미
신뢰를 약속이 아니라 검증으로 제공합니다

일반적인 게임은 내부 RNG를 사용해 유저가 계산 과정을 보지 못합니다. 반면 이 구조는 외부 공개 데이터와 고정 규칙을 사용하므로, 결과가 어떻게 만들어졌는지 누구나 따라가며 확인할 수 있습니다.

04 · 사용 가이드
검증은 3단계면 충분합니다
  1. 블록 번호와 표시된 값을 입력하거나 불러옵니다.
  2. 입력값이 Explorer 데이터와 일치하는지 확인합니다.
  3. 공식 적용 후 나온 결과가 화면 표시와 같은지 비교합니다.
Important Note. 블록체인이 직접 우승자를 선택하는 것이 아니라, 공개 블록 데이터를 공개 계산 규칙의 입력 재료로 사용합니다. 따라서 미래 블록은 예측하기 어렵고, 사후에는 누구나 같은 결과를 재현할 수 있습니다.

Platform

How the product works in practice

Block Beast Arena is a wallet-connected gaming platform designed for transparent round participation, deterministic result handling, and structured reward settlement. Users connect a supported Solana wallet, convert SOL into playable in-platform tickets, enter live rounds, select one or more options, and receive rewards when the final round result matches their winning participation.

The service is built to reduce operational ambiguity. Participation units, prize-pool formation, ticket usage, and reward settlement are separated into clearly understandable stages so users can review what they spend, what they entered, and how results are settled.

Core Product Structure

Wallet-first access

The platform is designed around direct wallet interaction rather than traditional email-and-password onboarding. Users remain responsible for signing transactions and maintaining control of their wallets. The platform does not take custody of private keys or wallet recovery phrases.

Ticket-based gameplay

Users do not participate in rounds directly with SOL. Instead, SOL is exchanged into in-platform ticket balance, and tickets are consumed when entering a round. This structure makes participation accounting, prize-pool calculation, and reward settlement easier to present and audit.

Round-based settlement

Each round has a defined participation window, final result condition, and settlement flow. Once the official round result is fixed, winning ticket volume is calculated, the reward allocation is derived from published platform logic, and eligible rewards become claimable or distributable according to the system flow.

Operational Design Principles

  • Clear separation between wallet connection, ticket exchange, gameplay entry, and reward settlement
  • Consistent round logic across participation, locking, result confirmation, and payout processing
  • Visibility into balances, transaction history, and round records wherever technically available
  • Operational controls for fraud prevention, duplicate participation review, and risk monitoring

What Users Should Understand

Before using the platform, users should understand that blockchain interactions may be irreversible, ticket balances are gameplay units rather than direct wallet-native assets, and rewards depend on official round outcomes and the platform’s published settlement logic.

Platform documents are intended to explain the commercial and operational structure of the service in a clear, service-ready format. They do not replace the Terms, Privacy Policy, or game-specific rules.

Fair Play

Rules, integrity, and participation controls

The platform is operated on the basis that gameplay rules, result conditions, and reward logic must be clear enough for users to understand how rounds are entered and how outcomes are settled. Fair Play principles exist to preserve round integrity, reduce abuse, and support equal treatment of legitimate participants.

Round Integrity

Each game round follows a defined participation structure. Users may place tickets only while the round is open. After the relevant locking point, entries are no longer accepted or changed. Once the official result input is determined under platform rules, settlement is performed against the recorded entries for that round.

Participation Standards

  • Users must participate through their own wallets and approved interface flows.
  • Users must review ticket quantity, selected options, and transaction details before approval.
  • Users may not exploit interface bugs, replay mechanisms, or timing inconsistencies to alter round conditions unfairly.
  • Any duplicate, automated, or manipulative behavior may be reviewed and restricted.

Prohibited Conduct

  • Bot usage or automated mass participation designed to distort round behavior
  • Exploitation of technical bugs, delayed rendering, or settlement timing gaps
  • Attempting to interfere with platform logic, round operations, or payout calculations
  • Coordinated abuse, fraudulent claims, or the use of deceptive wallet activity
  • Use of the service in violation of applicable law or platform restrictions

Result Handling

Round outcomes are settled according to the platform’s declared logic. Users should not assume that partial interface previews, estimated multipliers, or temporary round states are final. Only the official locked result and final settlement state determine eligibility and payout amounts.

Enforcement

Where abuse, manipulation, or suspicious participation is detected, the platform may investigate wallet activity, delay settlement, freeze claims, restrict access, invalidate affected entries where justified, or take other proportionate measures necessary to preserve operational integrity.

Fair Play standards are part of the platform’s core operating model. They are intended to protect legitimate users, maintain confidence in round outcomes, and reduce exploitation risk in a live wallet-connected environment.

Rewards

Prize-pool formation and settlement logic

Rewards on the platform are determined by round participation and official round outcomes. The rewards model is designed so users can understand how prize pools are formed, how winning participation is measured, and how final payout values are calculated.

Ticket Exchange and Playable Balance

Users exchange SOL for in-platform tickets. Tickets are then consumed when entering a round. Ticket balances are intended for gameplay participation only and do not themselves represent direct on-chain native-token balances.

Prize Pool Formation

For each eligible round, the prize pool is formed from the ticket participation attributable to that round under platform rules. The platform may apply fee separation, rollover treatment, or other announced mechanics depending on the live product configuration shown to users.

Winning Allocation

Where a round produces one or more winning entries, reward allocation is based on the official winning participation recorded for that round. If multiple winning tickets exist, payout is allocated according to the round’s published reward logic rather than informal assumptions or off-platform estimates.

Claim and Distribution

  • Some reward flows may be distributed automatically.
  • Some reward flows may require a user-triggered claim transaction.
  • Displayed estimates may differ from final amounts until settlement is complete.
  • Network fees, wallet approval requirements, and settlement timing may affect completion speed.

Rollover and Exceptional Cases

If a round has no valid winning participation or if a defined exceptional state occurs, the platform may apply a rollover, defer settlement, or process the round according to the applicable published rule set. Users should rely on the platform’s final settlement state rather than temporary visual indicators.

User Responsibility

Users are responsible for confirming they have entered the intended round, selected the intended options, and approved the correct wallet transaction. The platform cannot reverse final blockchain actions simply because a user misunderstood a round or approved an unintended transaction.

Rewards documentation is provided to improve clarity around prize-pool formation and settlement. Final outcomes always depend on the round records and official platform logic in force at the time of settlement.
